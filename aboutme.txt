Three years ago, I arrived at Michigan as a mechanical engineering student with 7 years experience in competitive robotics and design but little for software. At the time I was glad I wasn't a CS student and that my future industry was going to be relatively stable. With robotics being such a multi-disciplinary study, I naturally wanted to gain more experience in my weak points. 

Fastforward to today, ironically enough, I've become a software engineering student. Although I still love designing mechanical parts, I've found a new passion for software engineering. During these years I realized that the part about robotics and design I truly like is the engineering and innovation. Creating systems that don't just work but are also elegant and maintainable drives me to build more. 

To me, software engineering isn't just about coding the theoretical fastest algorithm but also making sure that the code is maintainable and elegant. Despite my short background in software engineering, I would fundamentally describe myself as forever-curious and innovation-driven.
